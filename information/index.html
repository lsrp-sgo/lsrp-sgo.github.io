State Government Office

The responsibilities of the Department of Motor Vehicles, the Bureau of Firearms, and the District Court are collectively merged under the State of Liberty State Government Office, which also additionally maintaints the Computer Aided Dispatch system.

Firearms

In order to legally own and carry a firearm, you must have a valid weapon license and registration. You can request a weapon license in the CAD. Please note that prior to requesting a firearm registration, you are required to have a valid weapon license already. It should also be noted that unless a firearm registration request is marked as valid, it is still illegal to carry it.

Traffic Points

Every time an officer submits a traffic violation in the CAD, they are supposed to fill out a form (if they actually do it, that's a different story...) maintained by the State Government Office. Their inputs are then reviewed, and traffic points are then automatically shown in our database. If you reach over 5 points, your driver license will be suspended. If you reach over 5 points without having an active license (yes, it happens..), you will be blacklisted from getting one. Suspensions and blacklists are issued for an indefinite amount of time until they are appealed. During the appeal, a judge will choose when shall the punishment end.

Businesses

In order to do certain activities, you may need a license. This is defined within the business regulations. Getting a business license will not only grant you a permit to conduct the activities you desire, but also allow you to act as an organization - that, however, comes up with some responsibility. If you have an active business license, your business may be subject to frequent mandatory inspections to make sure you are following all applicable regulations for your field of operation - for example, if you were to be operating a business with firearms, the DRO inspectors would make sure that you are selling only products that have been pre-registered with the state. All business matters are usually handled through tickets. The public may view the history of conduct and inspections or other records of every business, even if they no longer hold their business license.
